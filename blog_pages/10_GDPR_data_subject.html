The Data Subject

One of the most significant aspects of the GDPR is the creation of the data subject. The GDPR defines data subjects as:

an identifiable natural person is one who can be identified, directly or indirectly, in particular by reference to an identifier such as a name, an identification number, location data, an online identifier or to one or more factors specific to the physical, physiological, genetic, mental, economic, cultural or social identity of that natural person

In other words, data subjects are and individual people who can be identified in some way by data collected about them. Notably, the GDPR does not require that data subjects be entirely uniquely or accurately identified by their data and identification can include a wide range of information, including broader categories such as cultural or social identities.
