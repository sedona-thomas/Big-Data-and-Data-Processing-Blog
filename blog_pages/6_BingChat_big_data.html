Bing Chat and Framing Big Data

Bing Chat was queried to identify commonalities in how big data is described and returned the following:

Query: What is big data?

Big data refers to data that is larger, more complex, and more diverse than traditional data¹. It comes from different sources, such as computers, mobile devices, and sensors¹. It is hard to manage, store, and process with conventional tools¹. Big data can be used to drive decisions, improve processes, and create customer-centric products and services².

The term "big data" is often used interchangeably with "data science" or "data analytics"³. It has three main characteristics: volume (the amount of data), velocity (the speed at which data is generated), and variety (the different types of data)².

Bing Chat was much more specific in its descriptions of the main charactierstics of big data and similar fields of study; however, similar to ChatGPT, it neglected to contextualize the data rights framework of big data which describes the impact on individuals' personal data. Since both ChatGPT and Bing Chat were trained on some form of digitized text datasets, their reponses to the query "What is big data?" reflect the dominant patterns in discussing the topic of big data and highlight likely limitations in mainstream discussions of big data.
