The GDPR

The General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) is European Union law related to data protection and digital privacy. It was first adopted in 2016 and went into effect in 2018. The GDPR specifically covers the collection, storage, and processing of personal data. It is one of the strictest and most comprehensive legislation related to the protection of individuals' personal data and applies to any organization that handles the personal data of European Union citizens including those outside of EU member countries.

GDPR Additions to Already Existing Data Protectons in the EU

The GDPR was specifically implemented to replace the Data Protection Directive which was adopted in 1995, a time when data processing capabilities were signifcantly limited compared to today. The GDPR was designed to address the shortcomings of the Data Protection Directive by building upon the Data Protection Directive's protections of the free movement of personal data by creating a new framework for three main actors in the use of big data: data subjects, data controllers, and data processors.
